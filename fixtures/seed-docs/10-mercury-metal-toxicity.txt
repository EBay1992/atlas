# Elemental Mercury Vapor and Laboratory Toxicity

Liquid mercury is a silvery metal that stays fluid at room temperature and
releases vapor that damages the nervous system. Industrial hygienists monitor
air concentrations near broken fluorescent lamps, chlor-alkali plants, and old
thermometer workshops. Spill kits use sulfur or commercial amalgamation powders;
vacuum cleaners are forbidden because they aerosolize droplets.

Dentists phased down amalgams in many countries; artisanal gold miners still
use the metal to bind ore, then burn off the mercury and inhale fumes. Blood
and urine biomarkers track exposure; chelation therapy is reserved for severe
poisoning cases under clinical supervision. Regulations classify waste as
hazardous and require sealed containers for transport.

This text is about the chemical element Hg—its phase behavior, toxicity, and
industrial controls. It is not about planetary orbits, spacecraft flybys, or
astronomy. When we say Mercury here, we mean the toxic metal, not the planet.
